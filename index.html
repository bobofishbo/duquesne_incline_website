About the Duquesne Incline

The Duquesne Incline is a historic cable car system that has been in operation since 1877, offering visitors spectacular views of Pittsburgh's iconic skyline. Originally built to transport workers from the city to their homes on the bluff, it now serves as both a working transit system and a preserved piece of history.

Visit our upper station museum featuring historic photos and exhibits that detail the mechanical engineering and social importance of this landmark.

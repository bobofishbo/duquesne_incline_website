About the Duquesne Incline

The Duquesne Incline is a historic cable car system that has been in operation since 1877, offering visitors spectacular views of Pittsburgh's iconic skyline. Originally built to transport workers from the city to their homes on the bluff, it now serves as both a working transit system and a preserved piece of history.

Visit our upper station museum featuring historic photos and exhibits that detail the mechanical engineering and social importance of this landmark.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I pay for the ride? ▼

Exact cash fare upon arrival. There are no tickets, no advance payment, no reservations, and no debit or credit cards. The Duquesne Incline is part of the transportation system in Pittsburgh and uses the same cash fare system as the buses and trolleys. A change machine is available for your convenience. Having the exact fare in hand speeds up the payment process considerably.

Do Pennsylvania Seniors ride free? ▼

Pennsylvania Seniors 65 and older who present a BLUE or YELLOW PRT card or a Senior ConnectCard ride free; otherwise, the adult rate applies. The Duquesne Incline is required to request a Pennsylvania Senior Citizen Transit ID card as proof of discount eligibility, no exceptions, in order to be reimbursed by the Commonwealth of PA.

Are there restrooms available? ▼

Sorry, no restrooms. The Duquesne Incline has been part of Pittsburgh's transportation system since 1877, like a trolley or the cable cars of San Francisco.

When are the busiest times? ▼

Saturday's and Sunday's after noon are our busiest times. Expect to wait in line for about 10 minutes in both directions.

Is the Duquesne Incline wheelchair accessible? ▼

There are many steps to traverse from the lower station parking lot. The Upper Station, located at 1220 Grandview Ave, Pittsburgh, PA 15211, is wheelchair accessible. Our observation deck is also wheelchair accessible. The historic (original) Incline cars can accommodate wheelchairs up to 25 inches in width.
